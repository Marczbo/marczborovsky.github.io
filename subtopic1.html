Predictive Maintenance & Anomaly Detection

Webography Subtopic 1 – AI in Nuclear Power Plants

Source 1

URL: https://discoveraccelerant.com/ai-in-nuclear-plants/
Site Author: Accelerant – Energy & AI Solutions
Abstract: This article talks about AI and predictive maintenance in nuclear power plants to prevent equipment failures. The nuclear plant of the future operates under mountains of information from sensors, gauges, pumps and even cooling systems, but small incremental deviations are difficult for the tracking windows to notice. Yet AI models can continuously assess these streams, pick up patterns that are transparent to the eye, and holistically investigate deviations from unexpected behavior before anything deemed catastrophic occurs. The site offers a few examples of machine-learning tools used to assess temperature, pressure and vibration levels in the safety injection, reactor coolant, emergency diesel generators and containment systems before discussing how predictive analytics result in less downtime, longer aged equipment life spans and decreased total maintenance costs. In addition, the site acknowledges that safety comes first and this early detection is able to avoid any failures that could compound into worse situations for those working in a nuclear setting. This is a solid site because it discusses the why behind predictive maintenance AI and how it's crucial for these modernized operational approaches.

Source 2

URL: https://inl.gov/feature-story/artificial-intelligence-in-nuclear-how-computer-and-data-scientists-are-enhancing-the-industry/
Site Author: Idaho National Laboratory (INL)
Abstract: This is a journal article from the Idaho National Laboratory. This explores new discoveries from computer scientists and computer engineers in the artificial intelligence field. Since AI plays a part in systems level monitoring and reliability, this topic is highly connected to the INL organizational purpose. For example, much of the legacy nuclear fleet operates on analog instrumentation; therefore, less data readily available and performance indicators harder to assess. AI assists in legacy digitization reading in instrumentation and performance degradation/unusual operation performance indicators (yellow flags, red flags). This journal article connects to trained machine learning models in parameter detection from precursory data obtained in plants so operators understand abnormal behavior before they see (or feel) anything. INL AI research pertaining to this is improved inspection AI through robotics and enhanced automated data management for safe (and effective) operations. Ultimately, this journal article champions that AI should not take over operator jobs, but facilitate teams knowing failures could happen before they actually do because system degradation happens. This is an extra component for operational predictions and maintenance accuracy for the most efficient understanding of complex systems. This is a trustworthy source for additional insight into the applied research of the American nuclear industry.
