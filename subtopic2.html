Source 1

URL: https://www.nrc.gov/ai/externally-focused
Site Author: U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC)
Abstract: The U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission's site explains how the U.S. NRC is preparing for AI developments in nuclear facilities across the nation. It explains the agency's established "Principles of Good Regulation" and how AI will be evaluated for safety, transparency and reliability. It notes various regulatory concerns - explainability, security and compliance with federal safety regulations - that must be maintained even if AI makes such decisions. In addition, cybersecurity is a major factor since AI introduces vulnerabilities that poorly designed/unmonitored systems increase. The NRC explains real-time projects on AI, public workshops and an expert forum to better understand the good and the bad of AI in nuclear reactors. Ultimately, this site conveys that regulators must keep up with developments in the technology world and constantly shifting landscape but they also must maintain strict safety enforcement.

Source 2

URL: https://vcdnp.org/expert-papers-nuclear-security-supply-chain-ai/
Site Author: Vienna Center for Disarmament and Non-Proliferation (VCDNP)
Abstract: This article supports the notion of AI security risks and regulations in systems/supply chains. It talks about the benefits, naturally, of AI in systems - faster analysis, broader surveillance, better predictions - but then the need to assess how the vulnerabilities weigh against them. It emphasizes the cyberattacks, data manipulation, and interference to which AI is susceptible and how this impacts sensitive systems. It discusses how the AI complication undermines models for transparent/trusted decision-making (nuclear safety/security). It also explains how the presence of AI in nuclear facilities from previous systems means that only when AI comes into play with recommendations do the need for governance criteria, upgraded cyber requirements, international cooperation, and regulations-based systems approaches come into play. It even explains how supply chains will be compromised if AI systems are not deployed securely. This article gets to the risks and regulations that would go hand in hand with AI and nuclear systems.
