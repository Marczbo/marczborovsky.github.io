Source 1

URL: https://www.nuclearbusiness-platform.com/media/insights/role-of-artificial-intelligence-in-shaping-the-future-of-nuclear-power/
Site Author: Nuclear Business Platform
Abstract: This article delves into how AI is dominating the efforts of efficiency and performance improvement within nuclear power plants as of late. It observes that AI plays a role in supporting digital-twin systems, the virtual mapping of each reactor's virtual twin, allowing engineers to simulate action and testing energy production without interfering with the actual day-to-day developments in the plant. Moreover, it discusses how AI helps determine sensor-derived data that can improve fuel placement and evaluation in the plant and subsequently, refined control-room response with better thermal performance and comprehension. It continues by saying that the AI link is important because such algorithms can take the massive amounts of data and collate it in ways that better visualize what's not comprehensible by operators while working at a reactor. This reduces excess and increases efficiency and productivity. This also increases safety, as AI technology provides a more solidified edge to situational awareness. Therefore, AI will assist in taking over the next generation of these systems - especially small modular reactors that require such operational automation and data-dependent needs.

Source 2

URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9988575/
Site Author: A. Z. Aloraini, M. Alshahrani, et al.
Abstract: This peer-reviewed scientific review is from the last five years and covers recent developments in artificial intelligence applications for nuclear industry efficiency, optimization and decision-making. The review from the article notes AI applications and techniques - from machine learning and neural networks to evolutionary algorithms - that help model reactors, predict fuel burnup and optimize core designs. AI applications comprehend sub-second radiation and thermal-hydraulic data that would otherwise require extensive human computations. The more AI can help in scheduling, accident predictive analytics and multidisciplinary performance within systems the more nuclear plants can operate more easily with less operating costs. Additionally, the review notes relevant case studies that validate findings of AI in reactor modeling, fault prediction for scheduling and maintenance and reduced human error via improved diagnostics. The authors assume that AI will revolutionize nuclear engineering in the coming years with improved control strategies that allow for less uncertainty and more knowledge from findings due to quicker responses from large sets of data to previously impossible response times. This information will substantiate the need for academic contribution to AI's role in plant optimization.
